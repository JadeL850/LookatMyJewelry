“I also like to thrift for jewelry so I can style them easier,” Kayla tells me as she rearranges her most prized items. "I think there’s a certain fun collecting items that, you know, you can style however you like – you can build sentimental memories off of that too,” she remarks.

01. Charm Necklace

"I sewed this together"

“I wanted to make something personal, so I went ahead and
bought these charms and stones from the local hobby-lobby,”
Kayla explains, “and I had this leftover lace from this project
so I figured I should reuse it as a choker!”

Kayla is definitely a prime example of the ideal Parsons
Fashion Design student: resourceful, witty, and a handful
of personal style – even in the smaller details in life.

02. Hardware Necklace

"This was kind of my signature"

Within 10 minutes of our interview, I quickly notice that
Kayla is a huge fan of chunky pieces.

“This one really stood out to me while I was thrifting,” she explains, “this one originally had a magnetic clasp, which
was nice to know because I knew I wouldn’t loose it.”

“Ironically, it broke off though,” Kayla laments, “so I
don’t wear it as a necklace anymore.”

Although the necklace doesn’t serve its original purpose anymore, “it’s a great bag charm, because I can just tie this around wherever I want it to be, no one suspects it used to
be a necklace.”

It seems Kayla truly styles everything upon her touch.

03. Grandma Wong’s Pearl Necklace

"My sister and I chose this piece because its so dainty"

“When my grandmother passed away, our family decided
to gather her jewelry and belongings to split amongs ourselves,” Kayla remembers.

“I remember my sister and I made a beeline for this
necklace because it’s so dainty, and it’s really refined.”

“Seems to really suit you,” I note, “I guess you made the
right call.”

“Yeah, this adds a... different personal touch to my collection,” Kayla notes.

04. Elegant Earrings

"I feel she understood exactly what I like"

“These earrings were picked out by my best friend from high school,” Kayla explains, “I feel like I’m
not a huge on chunky jewelry, and I just like dainty, petite things.”

“She bought this for me as a surprise from thrifting
at a vintage store.”

I agree, surprised, “Yeah, I thought you bought this for yourself!”

Kayla nods, “Exactly, it’s almost like she understood exactly what I like!”
